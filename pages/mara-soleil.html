ABOUT
THE WORK

Mara's practice sits at the intersection of craft and concept. She learned risograph printing in a Berlin collective in 2016, and the medium's limitations — its registration errors, ink bleeds, and restricted palette — became the engine of her aesthetic.

Her editorial commissions are defined by bold silhouettes, unexpected color combinations, and narrative density. Clients commission her not just for execution, but for the point of view she brings to every brief.

She teaches workshops on risograph and printmaking in Berlin twice a year, and is currently working on her first self-published book.
